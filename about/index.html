My name is Marc Barrowclift, and I'm a professional designer and developer with over {{ site.time | date: '%y' | minus: 14 }} years practical experience.

Since the transformative moment my parents purchased their first iMac back in 2006, I made it my life's mission to deliver magic in my work like I felt upon booting up that iMac for the first time.

Upon arriving at Drexel University for my studies back in 2011, the fruits of that drive began to earn recognition: from collaborating with my professors to create the Guinness World Record's "Largest Video Game Display" at 2013's Philly Tech Week, to presenting the stylometry anonymization tool "Anonymouth" at 2013's Privacy Enhancing Technologies Symposium, and finally with helping land my team a coveted spot in 2016's senior design finals.

In my professional career today, I continue to deliver that same spark of joy in everything I do: from rock-solid backend services with understandable architecture and approachable documentation to lickable, delicious websites with a clear, human touch.

When I'm not applying those principles to software or design, I pass the time recording and editing a podcast with my siblings, playing tabletop games with friends at the venerable "game night", or crafting Magic the Gathering decks with my boyfriend for our next Magic tournament.

Thanks for stopping by, and I hope one of my works brighten your day.

This website was made possible with the help of many creative individuals and open source projects.
